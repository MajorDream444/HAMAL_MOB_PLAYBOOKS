# Model / Influencer Outreach Scripts

## Purpose

This playbook gives HAMAL clean, respectful, non-creepy outreach scripts for models, influencers, and high-performance personal brands.

The goal is to start conversations, diagnose system gaps, offer the Model Brand Pressure Test, and route qualified people into the Model Monetization System.

## Core Positioning

```text
Your face opened the door.
Your system keeps it open.
```

## Outreach Rule

Do not lead with beauty.

Lead with business structure.

Never make the person feel like they are being judged, hunted, or reduced to their appearance.

The tone should be:

- calm
- respectful
- direct
- strategic
- useful
- human

## Primary Frame

Models and influencers usually do not have a visibility problem.

They have a system problem.

They may have attention, DMs, shoots, content, and followers — but if opportunities are random and follow-up is weak, income stays unstable.

## Who To Target First

Start with people who already show signs of seriousness.

Best targets:

- models with 5K–150K followers
- fitness / beauty / lifestyle creators
- creators with brand collabs but weak backend
- Bali / NYC / Miami / LA / Dubai-based talent
- people posting professional shoots but with no booking flow
- people with “DM for collabs” but no media kit link
- people with scattered links or no email capture

Avoid first:

- minors
- people with no business intent
- people only posting thirst-trap content with no brand direction
- high-profile celebrities with heavy gatekeeping
- anyone where outreach could feel unsafe or inappropriate

## Outreach Ethics

- Do not comment on the body.
- Do not open with “you’re beautiful.”
- Do not imply they are failing.
- Do not scrape private data.
- Do not pressure.
- Do not promise deals.
- Do not use fake urgency.
- Do not mention likeness/tokenization early.
- Do not pitch AI first.

## Best Opening Angles

### Angle 1 — System Gap

```text
Random question — do most of your modeling / creator opportunities come in through a system, or is it still mostly DMs, referrals, and random timing?
```

### Angle 2 — Two-Week Test

```text
Quick question — if you stopped posting or working for 2 weeks, would your opportunities still keep moving, or would everything slow down?
```

### Angle 3 — Media Kit Gap


### Angle 4 — Respectful Pattern Interrupt

```text
I’ve been noticing something with models and creators lately — a lot have visibility, but no backend system behind it. Curious, is that something you’ve already built for yourself?
```

### Angle 5 — Bali / Travel Creator Context

```text
You look like you’ve built real visual presence. Curious — do you have a system behind the brand yet, or is most of it still content + DMs?
```

## Response Handling

### If They Say “What do you mean?”

```text
I mean the backend that turns visibility into structured opportunities.

Things like:
- media kit
- brand/collab tracker
- booking flow
- follow-up scripts
- owned audience capture
- content that points toward income

A lot of people have the front-facing brand, but the backend is still random.
```

### If They Say “I don’t have that yet”

```text
That’s actually normal.

Most models and creators are told to post more, look better, or wait for brands/agencies.

But the real move is building the system behind the visibility.

I run a quick Model Brand Pressure Test that shows exactly where the leaks are.
```

### If They Say “I have a media kit”

```text
Good. That’s one piece.

The bigger question is whether it connects to a full opportunity system:

media kit → outreach/follow-up → brand tracker → booking flow → content → audience capture.

Most people have one piece, not the whole system.
```

### If They Say “How much?”

```text
The pressure test is $250.

It gives you a clear score, top 3 leaks, and a system map for what to fix next.

If the leaks are obvious and you want help building the backend, that’s a separate install.
```

### If They Ask “Is this AI?”

```text
AI helps with analysis and speed, but this is not about selling you AI tools.

It’s about building a business system behind your visibility.
```

### If They Say “I’m Managed By An Agency”

```text
That can be useful.

But even with an agency, you still need your own backend:
- relationship tracker
- audience capture
- asset library
- content direction
- long-term income path

An agency can book you. A system helps you own more of the career.
```

### If They Say “I’m Busy”

```text
Totally fair.

That’s usually the exact reason to build the system — so everything isn’t living in your head and DMs.

The pressure test is light. It just shows where the biggest leaks are first.
```

## Soft Close To $250 Pressure Test

### Close 2 — Gentle


### Close 3 — Direct

```text
Based on what you’re saying, I’d start with the pressure test.

It will show the leaks fast.

If it’s useful, we can talk about building the backend after.
```

## Follow-Up Scripts

### Follow-Up 1 — 24 Hours

```text
Quick follow-up — I was thinking about what you said.

If your opportunities are mostly sitting in DMs, you’re probably leaking money in follow-up and tracking.

That’s exactly what the pressure test maps.
```

### Follow-Up 2 — 3 Days

```text
One thing I’d check for yourself:

Do you know your top 25 brand/collab contacts, their status, and your next action with each?

If not, that’s usually the first leak.
```

### Follow-Up 3 — 7 Days

```text
Still think there’s a real system opportunity here for you.

Visibility is already there.
The next layer is structure.
```

## Red Flags

Do not pursue if:

- underage without guardian/legal structure
- aggressive or disrespectful response
- asks for deceptive AI images
- asks to fake brand deals
- wants guaranteed income
- only wants free advice
- no serious commercial intent
